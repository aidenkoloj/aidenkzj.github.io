General information

I am a graduate student researcher in the Department of Biology at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). I am lucky to work with a talented, creative, and supportive group of computational biologists in Sergey Ovchinnikov's group.

My research is focused on the development of structure search methods to uncover structural and evolutionary relationships between proteins obscured by topological rearrangements (insertions, extensions, rewirings, circular permutation, etc.). See the CIRPIN project below for details.

I like to live out MIT's motto, mens et manus, as a mentor in MIT makerspace, leading weekly welding trainings and working on my own personal_projects.
